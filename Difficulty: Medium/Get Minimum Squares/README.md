<h2><a href="https://www.geeksforgeeks.org/problems/get-minimum-squares0538/1">Get Minimum Squares</a></h2><h3>Difficulty Level : Difficulty: Medium</h3><hr><div class="problems_problem_content__Xm_eO"><p style="text-align: left;"><span style="font-size: 18px;">Given a positive integer <strong>n</strong>, find the minimum number of perfect squares (square of an integer) that sum up to <strong>n</strong>.</span></p>
<p style="text-align: left;"><span style="font-size: 18px;"><strong>Note:&nbsp;</strong></span><span style="font-size: 18px;">Every positive integer can be expressed as a sum of square numbers since </span><span style="font-size: 18px;">1 is a perfect square, and any number can be represented as</span><span style="font-size: 18px;"> 1*1 + 1*1 + 1*1 + ....</span></p>
<p style="text-align: left;"><span style="font-size: 18px;"><strong>Examples:</strong></span></p>
<pre><span style="font-size: 18px;"><strong>Input:</strong> n = 100
<strong>Output: </strong>1
<strong>Explanation: </strong>10 * 10 = 100</span>
</pre>
<pre><span style="font-size: 18px;"><strong>Input: </strong>n = 6
<strong>Output: </strong>3
<strong>Explanation = </strong>1 * 1 + 1 * 1 + 2 * 2 = 6</span> </pre>
