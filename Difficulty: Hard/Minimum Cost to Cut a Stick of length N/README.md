<h2><a href="https://www.geeksforgeeks.org/problems/minimum-cost-to-cut-a-stick-of-length-n/1">Minimum Cost to Cut a Stick of length N</a></h2><h3>Difficulty Level : Difficulty: Hard</h3><hr><div class="problems_problem_content__Xm_eO"><p data-start="513" data-end="725"><span style="font-family: 'andale mono', monospace; font-size: 14pt;">You are given a wooden stick of length <strong><code data-start="552" data-end="555">n</code></strong>, labeled from <strong><code data-start="570" data-end="573">0</code></strong> to <strong><code data-start="577" data-end="580">n</code></strong>. You are also given an integer array <strong><code data-start="618" data-end="626">cuts[]</code></strong>, where each element <strong><code data-start="647" data-end="656">cuts[i]</code></strong> represents a position along the stick at which you can make a cut.<br></span><span style="font-family: 'andale mono', monospace; font-size: 14pt;">Each cut costs an amount equal to the <strong data-start="765" data-end="798">length of the stick being cut</strong> at that moment. After performing a cut, the stick is divided into two smaller sticks.<br></span><span style="font-family: 'andale mono', monospace; font-size: 14pt;">You can perform the cuts in <strong data-start="916" data-end="929">any order</strong>. Your task is to determine the <strong data-start="961" data-end="983">minimum total cost</strong> required to perform all the cuts.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size: 14pt; font-family: 'andale mono', monospace;"><strong>Example:<br></strong></span></p>
<pre><span style="font-size: 14pt; font-family: 'andale mono', monospace;"><strong>Input:</strong> n = 10, cuts[] = [2, 4, 7]</span><br><span style="font-size: 14pt; font-family: 'andale mono', monospace;"><strong>Output:</strong> 20</span><br><span style="font-family: 'andale mono', monospace; font-size: 14pt;"><strong>Explanation:</strong> <br>    <img src="https://media.geeksforgeeks.org/img-practice/prod/addEditProblem/915513/Web/Other/blobid0_1762855207.webp" width="532" height="266"><br><span style="box-sizing: border-box; color: #273932; letter-spacing: 0.21px; background-color: #f9f9f9;">If we cut the stick in the order [4, 2, 7], the cost will be 10 + 4 + 6 = 20, which is the minimum total cost.</span></span></pre>
<pre><span style="font-size: 14pt; font-family: 'andale mono', monospace;"><strong>Input:</strong> n = 8, cuts[] = [1, 6, 3, 5]</span><br><span style="font-size: 14pt; font-family: 'andale mono', monospace;"><strong>Output:</strong> 19</span><br><span style="font-family: 'andale mono', monospace; font-size: 14pt;"><strong>Explanation:</strong> <span style="color: #273932; letter-spacing: 0.21px; text-wrap-mode: wrap; background-color: #f9f9f9;">If we cut the stick in the order [3, 6, 1, 5], the cost will be 8 + 5 + 3 + 3 = 19, which is the minimum total cost.</span><br></span></pre>
<p><span style="font-family: 'andale mono', monospace; font-size: 14pt;"><strong>Constraints:<br></strong>2 ≤ n ≤ 10<sup>6</sup><br>1 ≤ cuts[i] ≤ n - 1<br>1 ≤ cuts.size() ≤ 100</span></p></div><br><p><span style=font-size:18px><strong>Topic Tags : </strong><br><code>Dynamic Programming</code>&nbsp;<code>Recursion</code>&nbsp;
